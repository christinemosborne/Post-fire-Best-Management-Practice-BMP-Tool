Step 3 — recommended practices

About this tool

This tool helps land managers and watershed staff identify best management practices (BMPs) for stabilizing hillslopes, surface drainages, and stream channels after wildfire. Suitability scores (0–20) for each practice were developed from field guidance and BAER-style reference catalogs, scored per parameter and averaged across whatever site conditions you specify.

Very Good (15–20) practices are generally well matched to the conditions you've described. Good (9–14) practices may work with added planning or constraints. Not Recommended (0–8) practices are poorly matched and typically should be avoided under these conditions.

This is a planning aid, not a substitute for site-specific engineering judgment, permitting requirements, or consultation with an erosion-control professional.
